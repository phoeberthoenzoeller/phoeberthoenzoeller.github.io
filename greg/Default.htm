Welcome, from the DeKalb County Amateur Radio Club Incorporated!

 Located in North East Alabama our repeater stands on top of beautiful Lookout Mountain in Fort Payne Alabama. The DeKalb County Amateur Radio Club (DCARC), active for 25 years invites you to visit our web site and our community.

 DeKalb County was established January 9. 1836 from land that was ceded to the federal government by the Cherokee Nation and is named for Baron Johann Sebastain, an American Revolutionary war hero.  Fort Payne, the county seat’s name was derived from the fort that was built during the forced removal of Indians named for an Indian stockade built in 1838 and commanded by Captain John Payne.  Fort Payne was the camp for 900 Cherokees in July 1838.  The town was used as both a removal fort and an internment camp, and today it is recognized as one of the starting points for the Cherokee's tragic migration to Oklahoma, known as the Trail of Tears.

 Today Fort Payne is known as the Sock Capital of The World and home of the Country Group Alabama Band.  We in the Amateur Radio Community are just a small part of that hometown spirit.  And would like to invite you to step inside our web site and learn more about our club.
